Forest

A forest is a complex community of trees, plants, animals, and microorganisms. It creates its own microclimate, stores carbon, and provides habitat for countless species. Walking through a forest connects us to ancient rhythms.

Observation

Notice the layers: canopy, understory, and forest floor. Each level supports different life forms. Light filters through leaves, creating patterns that change with time and season.

Ecological Insight

Forests are vital carbon sinks and biodiversity reservoirs. They regulate water cycles, prevent erosion, and produce oxygen. Protecting forests means protecting the planet's life support systems.
